Scope 3 Carbon Accounting Tool Analysis

An interactive guide for the University of Exeter

➔ Executive Summary

This interactive dashboard evaluates carbon accounting platforms to support the University of Exeter's Scope 3 decarbonization strategy, aiming to find a user-friendly and cost-effective solution that avoids the pitfalls of past experiences. The core objective is to identify a tool that excels at supplier engagement, providing educational resources and actionable reduction plans for a diverse supply chain.

The analysis synthesized findings from a mandated list and independent research, focusing on eight leading tools. Each was evaluated against a weighted hierarchy of priorities: 1) Supplier Ease of Use & Adoption, 2) Cost (especially avoiding supplier fees), 3) Quality of Reduction Features, and 4) Administrative Ease of Use.

Based on this evaluation, Green Project Tech and Sweep are the top recommendations due to their robust, supplier-centric, and free-to-supplier models. Greenly is a strong third contender, with the SCSS Carbon Calculator identified as a valuable, no-risk tool for immediate piloting.

✓ Conclusion & Next Steps

A new generation of carbon accounting platforms offers solutions designed to overcome previous adoption barriers. It is recommended that the university prioritizes Green Project Tech and Sweep for in-depth demonstrations, with Greenly as a strong third alternative.

  1. Immediate Action: Launch a no-risk pilot program with a receptive group of suppliers using the Supply Chain Sustainability School's free Carbon Calculator to build momentum and internal experience.
  2. Short-Term Action: Schedule one-hour, in-depth demonstrations with the top three candidates: Green Project Tech, Sweep, and Greenly.
  3. Actionable Intelligence: During demos, confirm unequivocally that there are zero mandatory costs or hidden fees for suppliers to use the core platform functionalities.
