The Challenge: Decarbonizing Our Supply Chain

The University of Exeter has a vast and diverse supply chain. To meet our ambitious climate targets, we must move beyond simply measuring our Scope 3 emissions. We need to actively engage with our suppliers—from large corporations to local SMEs—and empower them to reduce their own carbon footprints. This evaluation identifies the best tools to facilitate this crucial, collaborative effort.

In-Depth Tool Analysis

Green Project Tech

A comprehensive platform supercharged by the acquisition of Emitwise, focusing on collaborative reduction.

Vaayu

A retail-focused powerhouse with a highly sophisticated, automated, and granular calculation engine.

NetXero

An accessible and affordable platform designed to remove barriers for SMEs.

Axiom

A broad sustainability platform with strong ESG and social value integration.

Supply Chain Sustainability School

A free, industry-led initiative for the UK built environment sector.

Pledge

A hyper-specialized platform for calculating emissions from freight and logistics.

Our Recommendations

Primary Recommendation

Green Project Tech and Vaayu are the strongest contenders. They offer the most advanced features for enabling supplier-led reductions and should be prioritized for product demonstrations.

Complementary Strategy

Pursue a "Partner" membership with the Supply Chain Sustainability School. This is a cost-effective way to engage our numerous construction and estates suppliers using an industry-standard tool they already know and trust.
